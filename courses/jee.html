About IIT JEE

The Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) is India's premier engineering entrance assessment, organized in two stages: Main & Advanced. While JEE Main is presently conducted at the national level, the Advanced examination is rotated among the IITs. With over 1.2 million students appearing annually, competition is intense. You are advised to check the official websites JEE Main and JEE Advanced for the latest updates.

Our Approach

Recognizing the intensity of this competition, we ensure a fine balance between focused IIT JEE preparation and stellar Board Exam results. By identifying each student's potential early on, we create tailored strategies to maintain steady focus and confidence.

Important Guidelines for IIT JEE

The Government of India has notified the following changes in the JEE pattern:

  • There is no weightage for 12th class marks in calculating the ranking for the JEE Main examination.
  • To qualify for admission into IITs, NITs, IIITs, and other CFTIs based on JEE ranks, candidates must secure at least 75% marks in their 12th class exams (or be in the top 20 percentile). For SC/ST students, the qualifying mark is relaxed to 65%.
