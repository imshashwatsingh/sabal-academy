AIIMS and NEET represent the benchmark for medical entrance examinations in India. Eligibility Criteria For NEET/AIIMS

AIIMS MBBS Examination

A candidate must fulfill the criteria given below before applying for AIIMS MBBS examination:

  • Nationality: The candidate must be of Indian origin. Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) candidates must submit registration proof under Section 7A of Indian Citizenship Act 1955.
  • Age limit: Must have completed a minimum of 17 years of age.
  • Education: Should have appeared and passed 12th board exam (or equivalent recognised by AIIMS Delhi).
  • Subjects: Mandatory to have studied Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English in 11th and 12th class.
  • Marks Required: General and OBC categories must have secured a minimum of 60% aggregate marks in the above subjects. SC, ST, and OPH categories are entitled to a relaxation of 10% (minimum 50%).

NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test)

NEET is an entrance exam for admission to about 54,000 MBBS / BDS seats across India. A candidate must fulfill the criteria given below before applying:

  • Nationality: Must be an Indian citizen.
  • Age limit: The upper age limit is 25 years (relaxed by 5 years for SC / ST / OBC categories).
  • Education: Passed 12th examination with subjects Physics, Chemistry, Biology / Biotechnology, and English individually.
  • Marks Required: Minimum 50% marks in aggregate. For SC / ST / OBC categories, marks obtained in Physics, Chemistry and Biology / Biotechnology taken together in HSC is 40%.
